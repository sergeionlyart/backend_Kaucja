<div id="tit_1" class="eli-main-title">

COUNCIL DIRECTIVE 93/13/EEC

of 5 April 1993

on unfair terms in consumer contracts

</div>

<div id="enc_1" class="eli-subdivision">

<div id="art_1" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 1

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">1.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

The purpose of this Directive is to approximate the laws, regulations
and administrative provisions of the Member States relating to unfair
terms in contracts concluded between a seller or supplier and a
consumer.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">2.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

The contractual terms which reflect mandatory statutory or regulatory
provisions and the provisions or principles of international conventions
to which the Member States or the Community are party, particularly in
the transport area, shall not be subject to the provisions of this
Directive.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_2" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 2

For the purposes of this Directive:

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(a)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

‘unfair terms’ means the contractual terms defined in Article 3;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(b)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

‘consumer’ means any natural person who, in contracts covered by this
Directive, is acting for purposes which are outside his trade, business
or profession;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(c)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

‘seller or supplier’ means any natural or legal person who, in contracts
covered by this Directive, is acting for purposes relating to his trade,
business or profession, whether publicly owned or privately owned.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_3" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 3

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">1.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

A contractual term which has not been individually negotiated shall be
regarded as unfair if, contrary to the requirement of good faith, it
causes a significant imbalance in the parties’ rights and obligations
arising under the contract, to the detriment of the consumer.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">2.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

A term shall always be regarded as not individually negotiated where it
has been drafted in advance and the consumer has therefore not been able
to influence the substance of the term, particularly in the context of a
pre-formulated standard contract.

</div>

</div>

The fact that certain aspects of a term or one specific term have been
individually negotiated shall not exclude the application of this
Article to the rest of a contract if an overall assessment of the
contract indicates that it is nevertheless a pre-formulated standard
contract.

Where any seller or supplier claims that a standard term has been
individually negotiated, the burden of proof in this respect shall be
incumbent on him.

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">3.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

The Annex shall contain an indicative and non-exhaustive list of the
terms which may be regarded as unfair.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_4" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 4

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">1.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Without prejudice to Article 7, the unfairness of a contractual term
shall be assessed, taking into account the nature of the goods or
services for which the contract was concluded and by referring, at the
time of conclusion of the contract, to all the circumstances attending
the conclusion of the contract and to all the other terms of the
contract or of another contract on which it is dependent.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">2.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Assessment of the unfair nature of the terms shall relate neither to the
definition of the main subject matter of the contract nor to the
adequacy of the price and remuneration, on the one hand, as against the
services or goods supplies in exchange, on the other, in so far as these
terms are in plain intelligible language.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_5" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 5

In the case of contracts where all or certain terms offered to the
consumer are in writing, these terms must always be drafted in plain,
intelligible language. Where there is doubt about the meaning of a term,
the interpretation most favourable to the consumer shall prevail. This
rule on interpretation shall not apply in the context of the procedures
laid down in Article 7 (2).

</div>

<div id="art_6" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 6

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">1.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States shall lay down that unfair terms used in a contract
concluded with a consumer by a seller or supplier shall, as provided for
under their national law, not be binding on the consumer and that the
contract shall continue to bind the parties upon those terms if it is
capable of continuing in existence without the unfair terms.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">2.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States shall take the necessary measures to ensure that the
consumer does not lose the protection granted by this Directive by
virtue of the choice of the law of a non-Member country as the law
applicable to the contract if the latter has a close connection with the
territory of the Member States.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_7" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 7

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">1.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States shall ensure that, in the interests of consumers and of
competitors, adequate and effective means exist to prevent the continued
use of unfair terms in contracts concluded with consumers by sellers or
suppliers.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">2.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

The means referred to in paragraph 1 shall include provisions whereby
persons or organizations, having a legitimate interest under national
law in protecting consumers, may take action according to the national
law concerned before the courts or before competent administrative
bodies for a decision as to whether contractual terms drawn up for
general use are unfair, so that they can apply appropriate and effective
means to prevent the continued use of such terms.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">3.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

With due regard for national laws, the legal remedies referred to in
paragraph 2 may be directed separately or jointly against a number of
sellers or suppliers from the same economic sector or their associations
which use or recommend the use of the same general contractual terms or
similar terms.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_8" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 8

Member States may adopt or retain the most stringent provisions
compatible with the Treaty in the area covered by this Directive, to
ensure a maximum degree of protection for the consumer.

</div>

<div id="art_8a" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 8a

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">1.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Where a Member State adopts provisions in accordance with Article 8, it
shall inform the Commission thereof, as well as of any subsequent
changes, in particular where those provisions:

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

—

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

<div class="list">

extend the unfairness assessment to individually negotiated contractual
terms or to the adequacy of the price or remuneration; or,

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

—

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

<div class="list">

contain lists of contractual terms which shall be considered as unfair,

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">2.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

The Commission shall ensure that the information referred to in
paragraph 1 is easily accessible to consumers and traders, inter alia,
on a dedicated website.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">3.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

The Commission shall forward the information referred to in paragraph 1
to the other Member States and the European Parliament. The Commission
shall consult stakeholders on that information.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_8b" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 8b

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">1.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States shall lay down the rules on penalties applicable to
infringements of national provisions adopted pursuant to this Directive
and shall take all measures necessary to ensure that they are
implemented. The penalties provided for shall be effective,
proportionate and dissuasive.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">2.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States may restrict such penalties to situations where the
contractual terms are expressly defined as unfair in all circumstances
in national law or where a seller or supplier continues to use
contractual terms that have been found to be unfair in a final decision
taken in accordance with Article 7(2).

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">3.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States shall ensure that the following non-exhaustive and
indicative criteria are taken into account for the imposition of
penalties, where appropriate:

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(a)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

the nature, gravity, scale and duration of the infringement;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(b)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

any action taken by the seller or supplier to mitigate or remedy the
damage suffered by consumers;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(c)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

any previous infringements by the seller or supplier;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(d)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

the financial benefits gained or losses avoided by the seller or
supplier due to the infringement, if the relevant data are available;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(e)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

penalties imposed on the seller or supplier for the same infringement in
other Member States in cross-border cases where information about such
penalties is available through the mechanism established by Regulation
(EU) 2017/2394 of the European Parliament and of the Council (
<a href="#E0001" id="src.E0001"><span class="superscript">1</span></a>
);

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(f)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

any other aggravating or mitigating factors applicable to the
circumstances of the case.

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">4.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Without prejudice to paragraph 2 of this Article, Member States shall
ensure that, when penalties are to be imposed in accordance with Article
21 of Regulation (EU) 2017/2394, they include the possibility either to
impose fines through administrative procedures or to initiate legal
proceedings for the imposition of fines, or both, the maximum amount of
such fines being at least 4 % of the seller’s or supplier’s annual
turnover in the Member State or Member States concerned.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">5.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

For cases where a fine is to be imposed in accordance with paragraph 4,
but information on the seller’s or supplier’s annual turnover is not
available, Member States shall introduce the possibility to impose
fines, the maximum amount of which shall be at least EUR 2 million.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">6.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States shall, by 28 November 2021, notify the Commission of the
rules and measures referred to in paragraph 1 and shall notify it,
without delay, of any subsequent amendment affecting them.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_9" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 9

The Commission shall present a report to the European Parliament and to
the Council concerning the application of this Directive five years at
the latest after the date in Article 10 (1).

</div>

<div id="art_10" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 10

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">1.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States shall bring into force the laws, regulations and
administrative provisions necessary to comply with this Directive no
later than 31 December 1994. They shall forthwith inform the Commission
thereof.

</div>

</div>

These provisions shall be applicable to all contracts concluded after 31
December 1994.

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">2.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

When Member States adopt these measures, they shall contain a reference
to this Directive or shall be accompanied by such reference on the
occasion of their official publication. The methods of making such a
reference shall be laid down by the Member States.

</div>

</div>

<div class="norm">

<span class="no-parag">3.  </span>

<div class="norm inline-element">

Member States shall communicate the main provisions of national law
which they adopt in the field covered by this Directive to the
Commission.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div id="art_11" class="eli-subdivision">

Article 11

This Directive is addressed to the Member States.

</div>

ANNEX

<span class="boldface">TERMS REFERRED TO IN ARTICLE 3 (3)</span>

1.   <span class="boldface"> <span class="boldface">Terms which have the
object or effect of:</span> </span>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(a)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

excluding or limiting the legal liability of a seller or supplier in the
event of the death of a consumer or personal injury to the latter
resulting from an act or omission of that seller or supplier;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(b)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

inappropriately excluding or limiting the legal rights of the consumer
<span class="italics">vis-à-vis</span> the seller or supplier or another
party in the event of total or partial non-performance or inadequate
performance by the seller or supplier of any of the contractual
obligations, including the option of offsetting a debt owed to the
seller or supplier against any claim which the consumer may have against
him;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(c)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

making an agreement binding on the consumer whereas provision of
services by the seller or supplier is subject to a condition whose
realization depends on his own will alone;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(d)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

permitting the seller or supplier to retain sums paid by the consumer
where the latter decides not to conclude or perform the contract,
without providing for the consumer to receive compensation of an
equivalent amount from the seller or supplier where the latter is the
party cancelling the contract;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(e)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

requiring any consumer who fails to fulfil his obligation to pay a
disproportionately high sum in compensation;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(f)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

authorizing the seller or supplier to dissolve the contract on a
discretionary basis where the same facility is not granted to the
consumer, or permitting the seller or supplier to retain the sums paid
for services not yet supplied by him where it is the seller or supplier
himself who dissolves the contract;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(g)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

enabling the seller or supplier to terminate a contract of indeterminate
duration without reasonable notice except where there are serious
grounds for doing so;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(h)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

automatically extending a contract of fixed duration where the consumer
does not indicate otherwise, when the deadline fixed for the consumer to
express this desire not to extend the contract is unreasonably early;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(i)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

irrevocably binding the consumer to terms with which he had no real
opportunity of becoming acquainted before the conclusion of the
contract;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(j)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

enabling the seller or supplier to alter the terms of the contract
unilaterally without a valid reason which is specified in the contract;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(k)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

enabling the seller or supplier to alter unilaterally without a valid
reason any characteristics of the product or service to be provided;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(l)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

providing for the price of goods to be determined at the time of
delivery or allowing a seller of goods or supplier of services to
increase their price without in both cases giving the consumer the
corresponding right to cancel the contract if the final price is too
high in relation to the price agreed when the contract was concluded;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(m)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

giving the seller or supplier the right to determine whether the goods
or services supplied are in conformity with the contract, or giving him
the exclusive right to interpret any term of the contract;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(n)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

limiting the seller’s or supplier’s obligation to respect commitments
undertaken by his agents or making his commitments subject to compliance
with a particular formality;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(o)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

obliging the consumer to fulfil all his obligations where the seller or
supplier does not perform his;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(p)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

giving the seller or supplier the possibility of transferring his rights
and obligations under the contract, where this may serve to reduce the
guarantees for the consumer, without the latter’s agreement;

</div>

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

(q)

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

excluding or hindering the consumer’s right to take legal action or
exercise any other legal remedy, particularly by requiring the consumer
to take disputes exclusively to arbitration not covered by legal
provisions, unduly restricting the evidence available to him or imposing
on him a burden of proof which, according to the applicable law, should
lie with another party to the contract.

</div>

</div>

2.   <span class="boldface"> <span class="boldface">Scope of
subparagraphs (g), (j) and (l)</span> </span>

<div style="margin-left: 30pt; text-indent: -30pt">

(a)<span style="word-spacing: 18pt"> </span>Subparagraph (g) is without
hindrance to terms by which a supplier of financial services reserves
the right to terminate unilaterally a contract of indeterminate duration
without notice where there is a valid reason, provided that the supplier
is required to inform the other contracting party or parties thereof
immediately.

</div>

<div style="margin-left: 30pt; text-indent: -30pt">

(b)<span style="word-spacing: 18pt"> </span>Subparagraph (j) is without
hindrance to terms under which a supplier of financial services reserves
the right to alter the rate of interest payable by the consumer or due
to the latter, or the amount of other charges for financial services
without notice where there is a valid reason, provided that the supplier
is required to inform the other contracting party or parties thereof at
the earliest opportunity and that the latter are free to dissolve the
contract immediately.

</div>

Subparagraph (j) is also without hindrance to terms under which a seller
or supplier reserves the right to alter unilaterally the conditions of a
contract of indeterminate duration, provided that he is required to
inform the consumer with reasonable notice and that the consumer is free
to dissolve the contract.

<div style="margin-left: 30pt; text-indent: -30pt">

(c)<span style="word-spacing: 18pt"> </span>Subparagraphs (g), (j) and
(l) do not apply to:

</div>

<div class="grid-container grid-list">

<div class="list grid-list-column-1">

—

</div>

<div class="grid-list-column-2">

<div class="list">

transactions in transferable securities, financial instruments and other
products or services where the price is linked to fluctuations in a
stock exchange quotation or index or a financial market rate that the
seller or supplier does not control;

</div>
